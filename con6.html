AA Concept VI
Delegation in Action

How Alcoholics Anonymous balances the democratic voice of its groups with the realistic, daily legal and financial operations required to protect our global fellowship.

The Official Text

"On behalf of A.A. as a whole, our General Service Conference has the principal responsibility for the maintenance of our world services, and it traditionally has the final decision respecting large matters of general policy and finance. But the Conference also recognizes that the chief initiative and the active responsibility in most of these matters should be exercised primarily by the Trustee members of the Conference when they act among themselves as the General Service Board of Alcoholics Anonymous."

— The Twelve Concepts for World Service, authored by Bill W. in 1962.

The Ultimate Breakdown

Concept VI establishes the vital operational boundary in AA. While the Conference acts as the "stockholders" representing the groups, the Trustees of the General Service Board act as the "directors," holding the real capability and legal duty to execute day-to-day decisions.

Why This is Necessary

The Problem of Scale

The General Service Conference consists of over 90 delegates who meet only once a year for a single week. It is physically impossible for them to sign office leases, negotiate distribution agreements, or run day-to-day staff operations.

The Operational Solution

By delegating direct execution and legal accountability to the General Service Board, the fellowship is preserved, legal liabilities are isolated, and the routine tasks are handled safely 365 days a year.

Key Dynamic

"If the Conference micromanages the Board, the system freezes. If the Board ignores the Conference, they betray the groups. Balance is everything."

Demystifying the Key Terms

Translating corporate-sounding legal terms into clear and operational plain English.

Corporate Term 1

Chief Initiative

In Plain English

"The duty to step forward, speak up, and propose solutions first."

Trustees shouldn't sit idly waiting for the annual General Service Conference to dictate instructions.

How they practice this:

  • Identifying drops in contributions and drafting structural budgets.
  • Spotting service gaps (e.g., modernizing digital literature delivery).
  • Drafting operational proposals to submit back to the Conference for approval.

Corporate Term 2

Active Responsibility

In Plain English

"The duty to execute the actual work and be directly accountable for its outcome."

Trustees hold the executive, fiscal, and legal accountability. They cannot deflect real-world failures back to the groups.

How they practice this:

  • Signing legally-binding corporate leases for global offices.
  • Overseeing AA publications and employing office staff.
  • Taking the legal and moral blame if a project goes over budget or hits a crisis.
02

Policy vs. Execution: The Essential Boundary

General Policy (The Conference's Job)

Decides what AA as a whole stands for. Establishes the spiritual direction, votes on broad budgets, and approves major changes to foundational literature.

Executive Action (The Trustees' Job)

Decides how to execute those policies safely, within budget, and under compliance. Handles the daily, gritty administration needed to keep AA running globally.

The Inverted Pyramid of AA

Power in AA flows from the bottom up. At the top sit the groups, holding absolute authority; at the bottom, the GSB executes operations on their behalf.
